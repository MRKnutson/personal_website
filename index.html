Welcome

Hi! I'm Michael Knutson, a biologist turned programmer. Read more about me below or enter your email if you would like to contact me!

Working as a biologist sparked my desire to talk to computers.

I've always been intrigued by computer languages but growing up I never dedicated the time to really learn them. After becoming a biologist and working in the fisheries field for several years I was encouraged by a supervisor to pursue my M.S. in Fisheries. Although it was likely not my supervisor's intent, the skill that I found the most passion for while obtaining my M.S. was writing data analysis scripts in the open-source program R. This ultimately drove me down the rabbit hole of becoming more and more interested in computer languages. While working with the Alaska Dept. of Fish Game, I worked alongside another biologist and our resident programmer to develop new applications that allowed direct entry of biological measurements into a local database that we deploy on our Fish and Game research vessel.

I became so captivated with program development that I began learning some basic HTML, CSS, and javascript on my own. Ultimately, I decided that my interests are in developing useful tools to help companies and organizations gather, store, and analyze data and metrics. Now I am scheduled to attend the DevPoint labs web development program beginning November 2021.

My goal is to utilize the skills that I gain through DevPoint and my career to help improve the data collection and storage methods for the people I work with while creating useful and interesting interfaces for the front-end user.
